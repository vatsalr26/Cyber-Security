What is an OTP?

An OTP (One-Time Password) is a unique, time-sensitive code sent to your email or phone. It is used to verify your identity and ensure secure access to your account.

How Does OTP Work?

When you request an OTP, a random code is generated and sent to your registered email or phone. You must enter this code within a limited time to complete the verification process.

Why Use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)?

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of security to your account. It combines something you know (your password) with something you have (an OTP) to verify your identity.

Benefits of 2FA

Prevents unauthorized access, protects sensitive information, reduces phishing risks, and enhances overall account security.

Interesting Facts About OTP

OTP codes are typically valid for only 5-10 minutes to prevent misuse. They are widely used in banking, email services, and social media platforms.

Tips for Using 2FA

Always enable 2FA on accounts that support it. Use a secure email address, never share your OTP, and consider using authenticator apps for added security.
